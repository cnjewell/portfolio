Hey there TF!

This here is my flask project for Unit 2. I've been working ahead of where the class is at the moment, so I've done things a bit differently than the Unit 2 assignments asked. Instead of doing individual assignments (Web Caesar, User Signup, Build-a-Blog, and Blogz) I've combined them all into one flask app. You'll find functionality meeting, and sometimes exceeding, the requirements laid out in the assignments.

Please note, however, that I have deviated from the suggested methods of completion in some areas. One notable exception is the use of url query strings (i.e. "?post_id=2"). I have not implemented them in my project. I hope you'll forgive me! While I do understand their importance in applications like Google's searches, I felt as if I may use this opportunity to implement a cleaner, more beautiful url configuration. You'll find that the urls are organized the following manner:

  • /blog and /blog/username direct the user to a list of all blog authors on the site, as requested by the assignment, and to a list of all posts by a particular author.
  • /blog/posts and /blog/posts/# direct the user to a list of all posts and to individual posts by their primary key number.
  • Both author and post url routers handle missing posts and authors with redierects and flash messages.
  • Both author and post url routers are designed not to pass in data that may be a SQL injection.

I also took the opportunity to implement database migrations. Before the class started I worked briefly with Django, which implements them by default. It seemed strange that Unit 2 didn't cover what seemed like such an important subject, so I took this as an opportunity to teach myself! I installed Flask-Migrate and added the username field to my User model without DROP'ping my entire database. That ended up being a tremendously satisfying experience, getting all that to play nice with what I had already written.

I hope you'll forgive the deviations from the assignment.
